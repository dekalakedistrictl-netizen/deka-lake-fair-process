Deka Lake / Interlakes public engagement request

Deka Lake needs fair rules before enforcement escalates.

Deka Lake property owners should not have to learn the rules through enforcement. Ask the CRD and MLA Lorne Doerkson for meaningful engagement, clear guidance, and a practical path forward.

Why this matters

Deka Lake is a rural, seasonal, recreational lake community. For decades, and across generations, families have used, cared for, and maintained properties here in ways that reflect the area’s history, geography, lot patterns, climate, and practical recreational use.

Many property owners are concerned that enforcement is moving faster than meaningful public engagement, plain-language guidance, and realistic transition pathways. A single mid-week meeting, without broad notice, virtual access, and clear options for written input, risks excluding the very people most affected — including seasonal property owners, working families, out-of-area owners, and families who use their properties seasonally.

This is not about rejecting rules. Safety, sewage, riparian protection, wildfire risk, environmental protection, and responsible land use matter. This is about fairness.

The concern is that the regulatory framework has become increasingly disconnected from the lived reality of Deka Lake. Expectations have not been clearly reconciled with the area’s rural recreational character, consistently communicated, consistently understood, or consistently enforced over time. Property owners should not have to learn the rules through enforcement, and the CRD should work with the community before escalating enforcement.

What we are asking for

  1. A dedicated Deka Lake / Interlakes public meeting that is broadly advertised, scheduled with adequate notice, and includes virtual attendance options and clear written-submission pathways so seasonal property owners, out-of-area owners, working families, seniors, and others unable to attend a single in-person meeting can participate meaningfully.
  2. Plain-language guidance for bunkies, small cabins, accessory buildings, RV covers, trailer roof structures, decks, shipping containers, outhouses, greywater/septic considerations, recreational vehicles, and seasonal-use structures.
  3. Deferral of non-urgent enforcement against existing seasonal-use structures while planning and bylaw review processes are underway, except where there is a verified safety, sewage, riparian, wildfire, environmental, or commercial-use concern.
  4. Accessible, fair, and realistic transition pathways for existing seasonal-use structures, with clear criteria, practical options, reasonable timelines, and an approach that reflects the history, lot patterns, seasonal use, weather constraints, contractor availability, and rural reality of the Deka Lake / Interlakes area.
  5. Risk-based enforcement focused on actual hazards and material impacts.
