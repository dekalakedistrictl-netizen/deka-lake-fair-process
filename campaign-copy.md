# Campaign copy

## Campaign title

Deka Lake Needs Fair Rules Before Enforcement Escalates

## Headline

Tell the CRD: Deka Lake property owners should not have to learn the rules through enforcement.

## Intro copy

Deka Lake is a rural, seasonal, recreational lake community. For decades, people have used and maintained properties here in ways that reflect the area's history, geography, lot patterns, climate, and practical recreational use.

Now, many property owners are concerned that CRD enforcement is moving faster than public engagement, plain-language guidance, and a realistic transition process.

This is not about rejecting rules. Safety, sewage, riparian protection, wildfire risk, environmental protection, and responsible land use matter.

This is about fairness.

The concern is that the regulatory framework has become increasingly disconnected from the lived reality of Deka Lake. Expectations have not been consistently communicated, consistently understood, or consistently enforced. The CRD should work with the community before escalating enforcement.

Deka Lake property owners should not have to learn the rules through enforcement.

## Standard email body

Dear MLA Doerkson, CRD Planning Services, Director de Vries, and CRD administration,

I am writing as someone connected to the Deka Lake / Interlakes area.

Deka Lake is a rural, seasonal, recreational lake community. People who own, visit, and care for property here pay taxes, maintain land, support South Cariboo businesses, and contribute to the local economy and community.

I am concerned that CRD enforcement is moving ahead without enough meaningful local engagement, plain-language guidance, or recognition of how this area has actually developed and been used over time.

For decades, many Deka Lake properties have been used in practical rural recreational ways: seasonal stays, family use, RV use, small accessory structures, bunkies, decks, storage, weather protection, and other improvements that make lake properties usable. These uses reflect the area's history, geography, lot patterns, climate, and seasonal character.

The issue is not whether rules should exist. Clear rules are important for safety, sewage, riparian protection, wildfire risk, environmental protection, and responsible land use.

The issue is fairness. The regulatory framework has become increasingly disconnected from the lived reality of Deka Lake. Expectations have not been consistently communicated, consistently understood, or consistently enforced. Property owners should not have to learn the rules through enforcement.

If CRD expectations have changed, hardened, or are now being applied more aggressively, the CRD should say so openly, explain the rules clearly, and work with the community before escalating enforcement action. I am also asking MLA Doerkson to support a fair, transparent process for rural recreational property owners.

I am asking the CRD to:

1. Hold a dedicated Deka Lake / Interlakes public meeting before further enforcement escalation or bylaw changes affecting rural recreational properties.

2. Recognise Deka Lake's rural, seasonal, recreational character in the OCP, zoning, building bylaw, and enforcement policy review processes.

3. Publish plain-language guidance on bunkies, small cabins, accessory buildings, RV covers, trailer roof structures, decks, shipping containers, outhouses, greywater/septic considerations, recreational vehicles, and seasonal-use structures.

4. Defer non-urgent enforcement against existing seasonal-use structures while planning and bylaw review processes are underway, except where there is a verified safety, sewage, riparian, wildfire, environmental, or commercial-use concern.

5. Create a fair, case-by-case transition pathway for existing seasonal-use structures, with clear criteria, realistic timelines, and practical options for property owners.

6. Focus enforcement on actual hazards and material impacts, not ordinary seasonal enjoyment of private rural land.

7. Release public information about enforcement trends in Area L and Deka Lake, including whether files are complaint-driven, staff-initiated, Area Director-initiated, or opened through proactive patrol.

8. Confirm whether any boundary review, service-area review, tax-base study, municipal expansion discussion, or intergovernmental planning process is connected to current enforcement priorities in Deka Lake or Area L.

Deka Lake property owners are not asking for special treatment. We are asking for fair treatment.

The CRD should not treat the area's rural recreational history as a compliance problem to be enforced away. The better path is to work with the community, clarify expectations, provide fair transition options, and focus enforcement on genuine risks.

Please treat this message as a request for meaningful public engagement and as an early submission into any OCP, zoning, building bylaw, or enforcement policy review affecting Deka Lake and the Interlakes.

Sincerely,
